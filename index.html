Studio

Kumana comes from Kulture, Making, and Narratives.

KU from kulture. MA from making. NA from narratives.

Kumana exists at the meeting point of people, place, craft, and story. We believe design is not only about what is built, but about what is carried forward. Every space holds a culture. Every object reveals a way of making. Every project has a narrative that shapes how people belong, gather, move, work, and remember.

At our core, we are architectural designers. We design buildings, interiors, urban spaces, and event environments. But our practice extends into the conceptual space, where architecture becomes a tool for creative strategy, cultural expression, and human experience.

Vision

Where the practice is headed.

Kumana didn't set out to only design buildings — and it isn't stopping there either.

Our work is already moving from architecture into landscaping, urban space, and cultural experience, because the questions we care about — how people gather, move, and belong — don't stop at a building's front door. Each new discipline we take on grows out of the same architectural instinct, applied somewhere new.

This isn't a one-person studio chasing every trend. Every project is carried by a small, dedicated team who stay with a client from the first sketch through to final handover — the same people who know the site, know the brief, and stay accountable for both.

Manifesto

We believe space is never neutral.

It can gather people or separate them.

It can carry memory or erase it.

It can make people feel seen, grounded, inspired, or disconnected.

Kumana designs from this understanding. We are interested in the relationship between culture and form, between making and meaning, between the physical environment and the stories people carry into it. We design for people. We design for place. We design for the future that culture is still becoming.
